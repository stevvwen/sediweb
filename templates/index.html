About me

I am a Master student in Computer Science at McGill University, MILA and CIM. I am currently supervised by Prof. Gregory Dudek and Prof. Doina Precup working on the intersection of Reinforcement Learning(RL) and Robotics.
  Previously, I was an undergraduate student at McGill University (2020-2024) majoring in Honors Computer Science. During my undergraduate studies, I interned at MILA with Prof. Doina Precup working on Continual RL (CRL). I also worked with Prof. Xue Liu and Dr. Jie Fu on various topics.



Research Interests

Broadly speaking, I am interested in adaptive systems where agents/robots/models can continually learn to handle difficult tasks. Specifically, I am driven by the following questions:
  • Why do SOTA RL algorithms fail in dynamic real-world applications?
  • How to design CRL algorithms in robotics to efficiently adapt to dynamic contexts?
  • How to propel RL algorithms from simulations to real-world robotics applications?

Outside the technical research, I am very interested in the philosophy of AI, and more general philosophy topics. They sharp your minds.
